What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple numerical measurement used to determine whether a person's weight is healthy for their height. It is widely used by doctors and health professionals to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

Understanding BMI Categories

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the standard BMI categories are:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater

Health Suggestions

While BMI is a useful indicator, it does not measure body fat directly. If your results fall outside the "Normal" range, consider the following:

  • If Underweight: Focus on nutrient-dense foods and strength training to build muscle mass.
  • If Overweight/Obese: Aim for a balanced diet rich in vegetables and at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week.
