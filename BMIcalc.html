Understanding the BMI Calculator

The Body Mass Index (BMI) is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of a person. It helps you estimate whether your weight falls within a healthy range for your height. The BMI is defined as the body mass divided by the square of the body height, and is universally expressed in units of kg/m² — using kilograms for weight and metres for height.

How It's Calculated

The formula for BMI is straightforward and works with both metric and imperial units:

  • Metric Units: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))²
  • Imperial Units: BMI = 703 × weight (lb) / (height (in))²

Our calculator supports both systems. Simply select your preferred unit, enter your height and weight, and the tool will instantly compute your BMI value.

Interpreting Your Results

Your BMI result helps you understand your weight category based on World Health Organization (WHO) recommendations:

  • Below 18.5: Underweight
  • 18.5 – 24.9: Normal or Healthy Weight
  • 25.0 – 29.9: Overweight
  • 30.0 and Above: Obesity

Disclaimer

While BMI is a helpful indicator for most people, it has limitations. It doesn’t distinguish between muscle and fat mass, so very muscular individuals (like athletes) may have a high BMI but low body fat. BMI should be used as a screening tool, not a diagnostic one. For a complete assessment, consult a healthcare professional who can consider factors like body composition, diet, and activity levels.
